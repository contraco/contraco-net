The Booking You Never Saw

When AI Agents Replace the Travel Portal

📅 September 16, 2026 📍 Leipzig, Germany 🎤 fvw|TravelTalk Kongress 🎤 Speaker: Frank Meltke

The Disintermediation Nobody Is Pricing In

A growing share of travel research, comparison, and booking is starting to happen without a human ever reaching an operator's homepage. AI agents are doing the searching, the comparing, and increasingly, the deciding. For an industry built around the journey from search result to checkout, that journey is quietly disappearing.

At the fvw|TravelTalk Kongress in Leipzig, contraco's own home city in Germany, Frank Meltke presents the three questions every travel company needs to answer now, before the booking chain finishes automating around them.

Three Questions for Every Travel Operator

QUESTION 1

Who earns money in a fully automated booking chain?

QUESTION 2

Where do customer data flow when no human clicks "Book"?

QUESTION 3

What must operators build now at the data and interface level to avoid becoming invisible middleware?

contraco's Own Backyard

contraco isn't traveling to a foreign market for this one. With offices in Germany, South Korea, and the United States, Leipzig is contraco's own home city in Germany, not a destination contraco is visiting for the first time.

A Framework Already in Motion

This talk extends contraco's broader work on agentic commerce, the structural shift already underway as autonomous AI agents move from passive research assistants to fully authorized buyers. Travel is simply one of the clearest, fastest-moving cases of a pattern playing out across every industry with a checkout.
